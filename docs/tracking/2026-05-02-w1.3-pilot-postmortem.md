## 2. ADR-0006 Scoring (Harness Contract)

ADR-0006 § 6 lists five pilot success criteria. Each scored against the actual evidence produced by W1.3.

### Criterion 1 — *"The first progress note written under the new schema validates against all citation rules without amendment."*

**Score: PASS.**

Evidence: `.claude/progress-note.md` (commit `15f5e74`) was written by `/handoff` and validated against all six refusal conditions in `.claude/commands/handoff.md` § 3:
- Header present (`session-date`, `author`, `prior-note-commit`) — ✓
- Single Next Step — ✓
- Next Step `gates: []` (empty list, mechanically resolved) — ✓
- Each Learning flagged: L1 `pending-promotion`, L2 `pending-promotion`, L3 `pending-promotion` (later promoted in `9ec585f` post-rollout) — ✓
- OQ1 has addressee ("for Pat / human at post-mortem time") — ✓
- No Blocker IDs to conflict (Blockers section is `*None.*`) — ✓

Each State claim carries an inline `*(cited: <source>)*` suffix per § 1 schema. No amendment was required at write-time to satisfy citation rules.

**Caveat (minor, non-failing):** ADR-0006 § 2 op-consequence 1 says "the progress note's Next Step section names the spec it is operating against (or 'no spec applies' with the same one-sentence rationale)." The W1.3 progress note's Next Step names the work item (W1.3 post-mortem) and file path but does not explicitly say "no spec applies." This is a borderline citation gap — the Next Step is operating on a post-mortem artifact, not a spec, so "no spec applies" is the implicitly correct annotation but it was not stated. **Recommended follow-up:** /handoff command should require Next Step to explicitly state spec citation OR "no spec applies because <rationale>" — folded into ADR-0006 § 4 refusal conditions as a 7th condition.

### Criterion 2 — *"The W1.3 close ends with zero `awaiting-human` blockers that the schema failed to surface."*

**Score: PASS, with one observation.**

Evidence: the progress note's Blockers section is empty (`*None.*`). Every decision the human had to resolve at the W1.3 close-out was structurally captured:
- The persona-mapping conflict (ADR-0006 § "no persona-file edit" vs. sprint AC requiring personas.md annotation) was captured as L3 with a documented reconciliation. The human reviewed L3 and overrode the ADR; the override applied cleanly because the conflict was visible in structured form, not buried in prose.
- OQ1 (retroactive-spec validity) was captured as an Open Question with addressee, and the human resolved it inline.

**Observation:** L3 was initially classified as a *Learning* (a fact discovered + reconciled) rather than a *Blocker* (a decision awaiting human). Under the schema's structural distinction (team-answerable vs. human-required), this classification was correct at write-time — the team had a documented reconciliation rule (the gotchas line "sprint plan must not contradict accepted ADRs") and applied it. The human's later override demonstrates that the *resolution direction* of such conflicts (defer to ADR vs. amend ADR) is human-discretionary, not mechanical. This does not mean L3 should have been a Blocker; it means a class of Learnings has a latent Blocker-shaped escalation path the schema does not currently name. See § 5 follow-up F2.

### Criterion 3 — *"At least one Learning produced by W1.3 is either promoted to its destination during the same session or flagged `pending-promotion` with a destination."*

**Score: PASS.**

Evidence (from `.claude/progress-note.md` § Learnings):
- **L1** flagged `pending-promotion: docs/process/gotchas.md § Process` with rationale.
- **L2** flagged `pending-promotion: docs/adrs/0004-feature-spec-artifact.md § Pilot Plan` with rationale.
- **L3** **promoted** in commit `9ec585f` (override + reconciliation live in ADR-0006 Rework Notes amendment 13 and sprint-1-plan.md W1.3 AC); L3's meta-pattern further flagged `pending-promotion: docs/process/gotchas.md § Process` with co-promotion link to L1.

Three of three Learnings carry the required annotation. The promotion mechanism is demonstrated to work end-to-end: L3 was authored, reviewed by the human, amended, and the destination edits landed in the same calendar day.

### Criterion 4 — *"The next session that resumes from the W1.3 progress note completes its Session Entry Protocol read in under five minutes."*

**Score: NOT YET SCORABLE — defer to first true /resume.**

Evidence: a true cross-session resume has not yet occurred. The current session continued from W1.3 close-out without a session boundary; the post-rollout amendment work (commit `9ec585f`) was a *within-session* re-read of the progress note, not a cold start.

Partial signal: the within-session re-read of the progress note for the post-rollout amendments completed in well under five minutes (the human's L3-override decision was applied to four files in one cycle, indicating the structured State / Learnings / OQ block was navigable). This is suggestive but not conclusive.

**Follow-up F1:** the next session that genuinely resumes from `.claude/progress-note.md` (commit `15f5e74` + `9ec585f`) MUST log read-time as part of its session-entry telemetry. If >5 min, criterion 4 fails and ADR-0006 reopens on its own merits per ADR-0006 § 6 "failure handling."

### Criterion 5 — *"The `/handoff` command's refusal conditions fire at least once during W1.3 (any of them) — proving the mechanical checks aren't decorative. If none fire, the post-mortem must show the conditions are reachable but the session simply never violated them."*

**Score: PASS WITH RESERVATION.**

No refusal condition fired during W1.3 — the progress note was written conformant on first attempt. The criterion's fallback clause requires demonstrating reachability.

**Reachability demonstration (per condition):**
1. **Header missing** — would have fired if any of `session-date`, `author`, `prior-note-commit` were omitted. The header was authored from the scaffold template at `docs/scaffolds/progress-note.md`, which makes the field set inspectable.
2. **More than one Next Step** — would have fired if the L3-reconciliation work (write the gotchas note + rebut sprint AC) had been listed as two separate Next Steps. It was correctly captured as a Learning instead, which is the schema's intended forcing function.
3. **Next Step gates contains an unresolved Blocker ID** — could not fire because Blockers were empty. The check is mechanical: `grep` Next Step's `gates:` list, `grep` Blocker IDs, set-difference. Plausibly reachable on any session that ends with an unresolved decision.
4. **Learning neither promoted nor flagged** — would have fired if L1 had been written without the `Status: pending-promotion: <destination>` line. The author had to consciously add the line (the scaffold prompts for it).
5. **Open Question lacks addressee** — would have fired if OQ1 had been written as "Is a retroactive spec valid?" without the "for Pat / human at post-mortem time" preamble.
6. **Blocker missing stable ID** — could not fire because Blockers were empty. The ID generator (`B1`, `B2`, …) is mechanical and would catch malformed entries.

All six conditions are mechanically reachable; the W1.3 author simply wrote a conformant note. **Reservation:** the *practice* of writing conformant notes from a clean scaffold may mean conditions 4 and 5 are most likely to fire under sloppy authorship; conditions 3 and 6 only fire when Blockers exist. **Recommended follow-up F3:** the next session's `/handoff` should be authored by deliberately omitting one field per session (rotating across sessions) to confirm each refusal fires in production. This is a paranoid confirmation, not a hard requirement.

## 4. Joint-Reopen Rule Application (ADR-0006 § 6)

ADR-0006 § 6 binds: *"If the post-mortem cannot cleanly attribute a failure to one ADR or the other, both ADRs reopen jointly."*

**Attribution check:**
- ADR-0006's evidence (the progress note, the `/handoff` contract, the schema validation) exists and is scoreable independently of ADR-0004.
- ADR-0004's missing evidence (no spec) is attributable to a clean structural cause: the pilot was scheduled inside the same Wave that authored both ADRs, which made the spec-precedes-implementation ordering impossible in honest authorship. This is *a flaw in the pilot scheduling*, not a flaw in either ADR's design.
- The missing spec did not contaminate the progress note's content. The progress note was authored against the W1.3 work item directly (not via a spec); its citation rules and refusal conditions are independent of whether a spec exists.

**Joint-reopen rule does NOT fire.** Attribution is clean: ADR-0006 evidence is scored on its own; ADR-0004 evidence is missing for reasons attributable to pilot scheduling, not to either ADR. The two ADRs are decoupled by this post-mortem.

**Recommendation for future dual-purpose pilots:** if a Wave authors both an artifact ADR (like ADR-0004) and a contract ADR (like ADR-0006) where the contract ADR's implementation is the artifact ADR's pilot vehicle, the Wave plan MUST sequence the artifact-ADR's scaffold-landing *before* the contract-ADR's implementation begins, OR the dual-purpose pilot MUST be split into separate pilots. This is a sprint-planning lesson, not an ADR amendment. Captured as F5 below.
